9feet.com Trail Update


Despite 9feet.com's recent troubles the 9feet.com trail is open to ride. In fact it's open to ride all year round - the only charge is that for parking or using the jetwash.

If you're looking for somewhere to stay then there are two B&B's in the valley, together with a campsite at Afan and there's also the excellent HoBo Backpackers bunk house a short drive away. More details in our FAQ section.

Phil Lee 28 September 2001

Welcome to the 9feet.com trail. Over the last five year a dedicated band of local mountain bikers have given up their spare time to build a hardcore mountain bike route at Afan Argoed near Port Talbot here in South Wales.

The trail opened in August 2000 to glowing reviews but in fact the route was far from finished. Even so, each weekend around 70 mountain bikers descend on Afan Argoed to ride the trail and to clear the cafe of tea and cakes !

Unfortunately this success has meant that those sections that were not completely finished have worn rather badly - not surprising considering the the weather that we've had over the last few months.

However, despite the appalling weather, the same dedicated band of mountain bikers have been busy extending and improving the trail. There are still some sections to finish off but the work should be completed by the end of May 2001. To date the project has absorbed some 3000 man hours of our own time, mainly weekends and evenings.

If you are one of the many people that ride the route each weekend and have not helped out yet then why not sign up to the email list on the right. It'll keep you up to date with developments on the trail and will notify you of trail building days where you can come and do your bit for the 9feet.com trail.
